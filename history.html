History of the Department

Founded in the mid-20th century, Harley Davidson University initially offered a broad liberal arts curriculum, with economics being one of the key disciplines introduced in the early years. The department’s history reflects the broader trends in the field of economics and the growing demand for specialized education in economic theory and policy. In its early years, the Economics Department primarily focused on providing foundational knowledge in economics, offering courses in microeconomics, macroeconomics, and basic statistical methods. The department's early faculty members, many of whom had earned their doctoral degrees from prestigious universities, were committed to providing students with a solid grounding in economic principles, while also encouraging them to think critically about the world around them. During this period, the department’s curriculum was largely based on classical economic theories, reflecting the prevailing economic thought of the time.
As the world economy underwent significant transformations in the latter half of the 20th century—marked by the rise of globalization, the growth of international trade, and the increasing importance of technological innovation—the Economics Department at Harley Davidson University began to adapt and expand its offerings. The 1970s and 1980s saw the introduction of more specialized courses in areas such as international economics, environmental economics, and labor economics. The department also began to focus on policy-oriented research, addressing issues such as inflation, unemployment, and economic inequality, which were increasingly relevant in both national and global contexts. In the 1990s, the department experienced a period of rapid growth, both in terms of faculty size and academic offerings. A key development during this time was the establishment of a graduate program in economics, which allowed students to pursue advanced studies and engage in original research. The department also became more interdisciplinary, with faculty members collaborating with scholars from other fields, such as political science, sociology, and business administration. This period marked a shift toward a more global perspective, as faculty members began to focus on international economic systems, the role of emerging markets, and the effects of globalization on local economies. By the early 2000s, the Economics Department at Harley Davidson University had become a leader in economic research and education, with a reputation for excellence in both teaching and scholarly contributions. The department expanded its research focus to include topics such as economic development, financial markets, and the economics of technology and innovation. Faculty members were frequently invited to speak at international conferences and contribute to leading economic journals, solidifying the department’s reputation as a hub for cutting-edge research in the field of economics.
In the 2010s, the department embraced new technologies and methods, integrating data analysis, econometrics, and computational modeling into the curriculum. This shift allowed students to engage with the growing importance of big data and analytics in the field of economics, preparing them for careers in both academia and industry. The department also introduced new interdisciplinary programs, such as a joint degree in economics and environmental studies, and expanded its partnerships with organizations and institutions around the world. Today, the Economics Department at Harley Davidson University continues to build on its legacy of academic excellence and innovation. The department is known for its commitment to producing well-rounded economists who are equipped to tackle the challenges of the modern world. It offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs, covering topics from behavioral economics to public policy, and continues to emphasize the importance of research, global engagement, and practical experience. Throughout its history, the Economics Department has remained dedicated to its mission of providing students with the knowledge, skills, and opportunities to make meaningful contributions to the world of economics. With a strong foundation built on academic rigor and a commitment to addressing real-world issues, the department continues to evolve and adapt to the changing landscape of the global economy. As it looks toward the future, the department remains focused on its goal of fostering a new generation of economists who are not only experts in their field but also leaders capable of making a positive impact on society.
